ENTEROCOCCI BACTERIA

Could rain be causing bacteria runoff into the beaches?

The thesis of this project is that enterococci bacteria levels can be predicted using seasonality, beach location, and the previous 5 days weather conditions. Currently, it is believed that bacteria are washed into the sea by rain runoff. A bacteria source, such as an open sewer or agricultural site, must exist on land to produce the bacteria. This model does not take into account sources and therefore it isn't a perfect predictor of bacteria levels. The balanced accuracy of the chosen model is 67%. Models with additional weather data can reach accuracies of 75%!
The model uses the most recent weather data from Scholes Field, the Galveston Island airport. To improve accuracy, the model has been restricted to the 4 city beaches along the Galveston urban area. These beaches are Stewart Beach, 25th St, 45th St, and 61st St. You can select specific areas along each beach.
